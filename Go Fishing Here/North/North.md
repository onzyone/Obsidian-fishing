### What to Expect

- **Species:** Lingering steelhead drop-backs (Beaver fishway, Nottawasaga mouth), resident rainbow + brown trout (Humber upper, Boyne, Mad), brook trout on smaller tribs.
- **Sunday tactics (post-storm slight stain on limestone, more stain on clay):**
  - **Beaver Thornbury:** float roe sacs through deeper runs below the fishway; small spoons (Cleos #2, KO Wobblers) in the Mill Pond mid-morning; centerpin or spinning + 8lb braid + 6lb fluoro.
  - **Humber upper / East Humber:** small spinners (Mepps #1–2, Panther Martin) in pools; worm under split shot for resident brookies on the walk-in stretches.
  - **Boyne Earl Rowe:** float worms or roe through deeper bends; small inline spinners in the head of pools.
- **Gear:** Beaver = 8'6"–10'6" ML steelhead rod, centerpin/spinning, 6lb mono or 8lb braid, 6lb fluoro. Humber upper / Boyne = 6'6"–7'6" UL/L spinning, 4lb mono. Bring rain shell + extra socks (post-storm = wet banks).
- **Cautions:**
  - **Saturday is a hard SKIP** — Fri 12mm + Sat 8mm = peak storm during the day.
  - **Pre-trip gauge check Saturday night** — refresh 02FB009 (Beaver) and 02HC003 (Humber). Want falling. If Beaver still rising or above 14m, switch to Humber upper or push to Mon.
  - **Bighead is already rising** — blown out for this run, do not drive there.
  - **Long-drive rivers (Saugeen, Maitland, Sauble)** — 2h+ drive + 3–6 day clear time = poor R/R for this weekend.
  - **Last Quarter moon (~36–46%)** = balanced; standard dawn-patrol applies.
  - **Sanctuaries** — Thornbury fishway, Nottawasaga reg-specific zones.
- **Regulations:** Verify on [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary). FMZ 16 inland season open Apr 25 – Sept 30 for resident-trout zones.
