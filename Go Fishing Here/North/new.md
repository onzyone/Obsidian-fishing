## New Spots — North (cluster: Cookstown / Innisfil, ~30 min radius)

Drive from 427 & Finch takes ~50–60 min via Hwy 400 to the Cookstown/Innisfil area; this cluster targets Innisfil Creek, a lower-pressure tributary of the Nottawasaga holding resident browns and staging spring/fall steelhead near the confluence. With the main Nottawasaga often crowded or muddy, Innisfil offers clearer water and easier public access across three distinct gaugeable sections; all spots are open for the May 1–3 dates (season opened Apr 25).

## Notes

- **FMZ 17 inland trout regs:** open since 4th Sat April; standard creel limits. Innisfil Creek browns are wild — consider barbless C&R.
- **Property lines:** road-allowance only at bridges. Don't cross fences.
- **Tackle swap:** small-stream brown trout = 6'6"–7'6" UL/L spinning, 4lb mono, small spinners (Mepps #0/1) or worm under split shot.
- **Sequencing for Sun May 3:** Hit Humber (Old Mill) at first light → bounce north to Innisfil Creek for resident browns mid-morning if main bite slows.
