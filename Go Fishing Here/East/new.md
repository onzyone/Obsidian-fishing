## New Spots — East (cluster: Newcastle, ~30 min radius)

Drive ~55–60 min via 401 E / Hwy 115 S. This cluster locks onto **Graham Creek**, a small, lightly documented steelhead/brown waterway that never made the year-round roster and offers the first real low-pressure exploration of the eastern Lake Ontario shoreline west of Bowmanville. All spots target the lower Graham system where road allowances and engineered structures hold fresh-run steelhead staging before they push into the lake or turn. With FMZ 17 inland open since Apr 25, the creek is fully accessible for May 1–3.

## Notes

- **FMZ 17 inland trout regs:** open since 4th Sat April; standard creel limits. MNR brook-trout headwaters are low-density — consider barbless C&R for upper Graham brookies.
- **Property lines:** road-allowance only on most pins. Stay in the road allowance, don't cross fences. Many upper sections are private farmland. Watch for Port of Newcastle subdivision restrictions near the drop structure.
- **Tackle:** mouth/drop = full steelhead setup (8'6"–10'6" ML, 6lb mono, 6lb fluoro). Upper resident water = 6'6"–7'6" UL/L with 4lb mono and small spinners.
- **Sequencing for Sat May 2:** Bowmanville (Spry Ave) at 05:39 dawn → bounce to Graham Creek mouth (Newcastle jetties) ~9am if Bowmanville crowds up → upper Graham (Concession 7) for resident browns mid-day if energy allows.
