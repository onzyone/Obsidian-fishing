### New Spots Explore

#### New Spots — West (cluster: Cataract / Belfountain, ~30 min radius, 50–65 min drive)

Headwaters and small-stream cluster on the Niagara Escarpment limestone — clears 1–2 days faster than the clay-basin lower rivers, and FMZ 17 inland season is open since Apr 25. None of these are on the year-round roster. Pair them with a morning hit on 16 Mile or Bronte and roll up here for a midday/afternoon finish on small water.

| Spot | Drive | Species | Why | Map |
|------|-------|---------|-----|-----|
| **West Credit at Cataract** (Forks of the Credit PP) | ~1h | Wild brown trout, brook trout | Headwaters, gauged 02HB001; limestone basin clears fastest after Fri drizzle; C&R only | [Forks of the Credit PP](https://maps.google.com/?q=Forks+of+the+Credit+Provincial+Park+Ontario) |
| **Shaw's Creek (Alton)** | ~55 min | Wild brown trout | No gauge — proxy West Credit; road allowance access at Mountainview Rd; very low pressure | [Shaw's Creek at Alton](https://maps.google.com/?q=Shaw%27s+Creek+Alton+Ontario) |
| **Black Creek at Limehouse** (02HB035) | ~55 min | Resident brown + brook | Small Credit trib, gauged but rarely fished; road allowance bridge access in Limehouse village | [Limehouse Conservation Area](https://maps.google.com/?q=Limehouse+Conservation+Area+Ontario) |
| **Eramosa River (Rockwood CA)** | ~1h 10 min | Brook trout, brown trout | Limestone Speed-system trib; gauged 02GA029; standard FMZ 17 limits; paid CA parking | [Rockwood CA](https://maps.google.com/?q=Rockwood+Conservation+Area+Ontario) |

**Notes:**
- **FMZ 17 inland trout regs:** open since 4th Sat April; standard creel limits. MNR brook-trout headwaters are low-density populations — consider barbless C&R.
- **Property lines:** road-allowance access only on most upstream pins. Stay in the road allowance, don't cross fences. Many upper sections are private.
- **Tackle swap:** small-stream brook trout = 6'6"–7'6" UL/L spinning, 4lb mono, small spinners (Mepps #0/1, Panther Martin) or worm under split shot. Steelhead rod is overkill upstream.
