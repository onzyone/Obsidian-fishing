# Explore Fishing Here

Spots worth scouting on a slow weekend or off-day. Lightly documented, low-pressure, or visually scouted from photos. Confirm regs and access before going.

## Newcastle / Clarington bluff creeks

Small creeks cutting through Lake Iroquois clay/till bluffs. Drop-back steelhead, residents, fall salmon push.

| Spot | Coords | Park | Drive | Why Explore | Map |
|------|--------|------|-------|-------------|-----|
| Lower Wilmot Creek (bluff section) | 43.9270, -78.5945 | Mill St road allowance, Newcastle (south end of Mill, near CNR crossing) | 55–65 min | Dual clay bluffs, classic small-creek bluff geology, known steelhead water | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=43.9270,-78.5945) |
| Graham Creek lower (photo pin) | 43.9244, -78.5738 | Bond Head Parkette / Cobbledick Rd lakeshore parking; walk up creek | 60–70 min | Possible match for the IG photo; small creek, low pressure, FMZ 17 open Apr 25–Dec 31 | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=43.924429,-78.573844) |
| Lower Soper Creek (Bowmanville) | 43.9000, -78.6520 | Soper Creek Trail parking, Roser Cres / Concession St E, Bowmanville | 55–65 min | Bluff section south of trail; proxies Bowmanville gauge; drop-backs and residents | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=43.9000,-78.6520) |

## Northumberland north-shore creeks

Lightly fished, often clear faster than larger systems.

| Spot | Coords | Park | Drive | Why Explore | Map |
|------|--------|------|-------|-------------|-----|
| Cobourg Brook lower | 43.9580, -78.1680 | Donegan Park / Victoria Park near mouth, Cobourg | 1h 5 min | Lake-run fish push in spring/fall; lower section through ravine | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=43.9580,-78.1680) |
| Shelter Valley Creek (Grafton) | 43.9700, -77.9700 | Wicklow Beach Rd / Old Danforth Rd allowance crossings south of 401 | 1h 15 min | Tiny creek, bluff/ravine, almost no pressure | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=43.9700,-77.9700) |

## Nottawasaga clay-bank tributaries

Photo geology also matches these. Inland alternative if east is busy or blown.

| Spot | Coords | Park | Drive | Why Explore | Map |
|------|--------|------|-------|-------------|-----|
| Pine River (Everett area) | 44.1830, -79.9470 | Sideroad bridge allowances near Everett (5th Sideroad, County Rd 5) | 1h 15 min | Clay banks, brown trout + spring rainbows; small intimate water | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=44.1830,-79.9470) |
| Boyne River (Earl Rowe lower) | 44.1370, -79.8470 | Earl Rowe Provincial Park (paid day-use) | 1h 5 min | Clay-bluff reach, brookies + browns; trails along river | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/?q=44.1370,-79.8470) |

## Notes

- Coords are best-guess pins (~50–100m); switch to satellite view in Maps to confirm before driving.
- Most road-allowance access requires checking signage on the day — private land borders these creeks closely.
- Update this file as you scout: add what works, mark dud spots so you don't repeat.
