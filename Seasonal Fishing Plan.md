# Seasonal Fishing Plan — Ontario

> All rivers within ~1–2.5h of 427 & Finch, Etobicoke.
> Season opener for most rivers: **Friday before 4th Saturday of April** (Apr 24, 2026).

## Annual Rotation by Phase

### Jan – Mar — Winter / Early Spring Steelhead

Fish are present but lethargic in cold water. Nymph deep, slow. Year-round lower sections only.

| Direction | Rivers | C&R | Crowd |
|---|---|---|---|
| **West (close)** | Etobicoke Creek (lower), Credit River (lower — Erindale, JJ Plaus), Bronte Creek (below CPR) | No | Med–High |
| **East (close)** | Humber (lower to Eglinton), Don (lower), Rouge (lower to Hwy 407), 16 Mile Creek (lower to Hwy 2) | No | Med–High |
| **East (far)** | Wilmot Creek (lower south of CNR), Twenty Mile Creek (QEW to lake — open all year) | No | Low |
| **North (Georgian Bay)** | Beaver River, Bighead River, Sydenham, Sauble River, Saugeen River, Maitland River, Nottawasaga (lower) — Georgian Bay tribs get early runners in Feb/Mar | No | Low–Med |
| **Southwest (Lake Erie)** | Big Creek (lower — open all year), Big Otter Creek (lower — open all year), Lynn River (lower — open all year) | No | Low |
| **Southwest (Niagara)** | Niagara River gorge (year-round, complex border regs — verify) | Partial | Very Low |

### Apr 24 – mid-May — Peak Spring Steelhead + Season Opens

Best steelhead fishing of the year. All rivers now open. Flows up, fish pushing hard.

| ⭐⭐ | **Humber River** | Good run, urban access; above Hwy 407 is sanctuary | No | **High** |

### Apr 24 – June — Brook Trout + Resident Trout Opener

Trout season opens Apr 24. Best flows and coldest temps of the year. Brook trout are prime right at opener.

| Direction | Rivers | Species | Notes | C&R | Crowd |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **West** | West Credit (above Cataract Falls) | Brook trout | Barbless, artificial; brookies concentrate in cold water above the falls | **Yes** | Low |
| **West** | Upper Credit (Forks, Cataract, Inglewood, Alton) | Brown + brook trout | Cataract Falls divides brook (above) from brown (below) | **Yes** | Low |
| **West** | Shaw's Creek | Wild brown trout | Clears 1–2 days after rain — best Credit blow-out fallback | **Yes** | Low |
| **West** | Speed River, Eramosa River | Brown trout, rainbow | Standard limits; landlocked — no steelhead/salmon; Eramosa clears fastest after rain | No | Low |
| **West** | Grand River (Elora/Fergus) | Wild brown trout | Opens Apr 24; 25"+ fish realistic; don't stress fish in heat | **Yes** | Med |
| **North** | East Humber River | Brook trout (upper), brown trout | Oak Ridges Moraine spring-fed; Boyd CA + Bindertwine Park access; low pressure | No | Low |
| **North** | Innisfil Creek | Brown trout, brook trout | Low pressure; verify fish ladder access with NVCA | No | Low |
| **North** | Pine River, Noisy River, Boyne River | Wild brown trout, brook trout | Standard limits; best early season when flows are up | No | Low |
| **North** | Mad River | Brown trout, brook trout, steelhead | Spring fish still present early in this window | No | Low |

### June – Sept — Resident Brown Trout / Dry Fly Season

Peak hatch season on the cold spring-fed rivers. Isonychia (June–July) is the most reliable hatch on the upper Credit. **Avoid C&R on Grand River upper in peak summer heat — mortality risk.**

| Priority | Rivers | Notes | C&R | Crowd |
| -------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Grand River** (Elora/Fergus) | Trophy dry fly water; Isonychia, Green Drake, Trico; 25"+ browns; fish early/late in summer heat | **Yes** | Med (Elora Gorge busy; Fergus/Wilson Flats quieter) |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Upper Credit** (Forks of the Credit, Cataract, Inglewood) | Hatch chart peaks June–Sept; Isonychia June–July star hatch; 5–6x tippet, careful approach | **Yes** | Low |
| ⭐⭐ | **West Credit** (near Cataract Falls) | Brook above falls, brown below; cold limestone water; closes Sept 30 | **Yes** | Low |
| ⭐⭐ | **Shaw's Creek** | Excellent fallback when Credit is blown out; closes Sept 30 | **Yes** | Low |
| ⭐⭐ | **Eramosa River** | Best clear-water fallback after rain; landlocked; Grand watershed | No | Low |
| ⭐⭐ | **Speed River** | Resident brown trout; no runs; landlocked | No | Low |
| ⭐ | Pine River, Noisy River | Wild brown and brook trout; summer low water — early morning recommended | No | Low |
| ⭐ | Beaver River (upper) | Cold Niagara Escarpment water; good summer trout | No | Low |
| ⭐ | East Humber River | Brook trout upper sections; closes Sept 30 | No | Low |
| ⭐ | Innisfil Creek | Resident brown/brook trout; closes Sept 30 | No | Low |

### Sept – early Oct — Chinook Salmon Run

Triggered by first cold rains of fall. Fish stack below dams. Watch the CRAA fish counter at Streetsville Dam for timing. **No Chinook salmon on Lake Erie tribs.**

| Priority | Rivers | Notes | C&R | Crowd |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Credit River** | Streetsville Dam + Erindale Park; CRAA fish counter tells you when fish are moving | No | **High** |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Humber River** | Strong salmon run; multiple access points | No | **High** |
| ⭐⭐ | **Nottawasaga River** | Lower section (below Boyne R.) — good salmon push | No | Med |
| ⭐⭐ | **Grand River** (lower) | Paris Dam, Brantford — salmon push lower river | No | Med |
| ⭐⭐ | **Saugeen River** | Chinook salmon + steelhead beginning; Southampton mouth | No | Med |
| ⭐ | Don River, Rouge River, Bronte Creek, Etobicoke Creek, 16 Mile Creek | Lake Ontario tribs; salmon move through all of them | No | Med–High |
| ⭐ | Oshawa Creek, Wilmot Creek, Bowmanville Creek, Cobourg Creek, Soper Creek, Shelter Valley Creek | East Lake Ontario; lower pressure salmon fishing | No | Low–Med |
| ⚠️ | Duffins Creek | **CLOSED Sept 1 – Oct 14** lower section — no salmon fishing here during the run | — | — |
| ⚠️ | Ganaraska River | **CLOSED Sept 1 – Oct 14** mid section — Hwy 401 to Jocelyn St closed year-round; no night fishing Aug 15 onward | — | — |
| ⚠️ | Twenty Mile Creek | Salmon push into Jordan Harbour area; open all year lower section | No | Low |
| ⚠️ | Niagara River | Some Chinook; complex border regs — verify | Partial | Very Low |

### Mid-Oct – Dec 31 — Fall Steelhead (Peak)

Best numbers of the year. Key trigger: river temperature hitting ~60°F after sustained cold rain. Fish **dropping and clearing** — wait out blown-out chocolate water (5–10 days to clear after heavy rain). Lower river sections only.

| Priority | Rivers | Notes | C&R | Crowd |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Nottawasaga River** | Peak fall run; best numbers of any Lake Ontario watershed trib | No | Med (long river — walk away from bridges) |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Saugeen River** | Premier Lake Huron steelhead fall run; Denny's Dam staging | No | Med (mouth busy; Denny's Dam area quieter) |
| ⭐⭐⭐ | **Grand River** (lower) | Paris Dam stacks fish hard in Oct–Nov; Brantford, Caledonia | No | Med |
| ⭐⭐ | **Credit River** | Good fall run; Erindale, Eldorado, Streetsville | No | **High** |
| ⭐⭐ | **Humber River** | Consistent fall run; lower river only | No | **High** |
| ⭐⭐ | **Ganaraska River** | Reopens Oct 15 lower; no night fishing; strong fall run | No | **High** |
| ⭐⭐ | **Duffins Creek** | Reopens Oct 15; good fall steelhead, lower pressure than Credit/Humber | No | Med |
| ⭐⭐ | **Beaver River, Bighead River** | Georgian Bay tribs; cold clear Niagara Escarpment water; lower pressure | No | Low |
| ⭐⭐ | **Maitland River** | Lake Huron trib at Goderich; murky after rain — wait it out | No | Low |
| ⭐ | Don River, Rouge River, Bronte Creek, 16 Mile Creek, Etobicoke Creek | Lake Ontario tribs; urban options if nothing else is fishable | No | Med–High |
| ⭐ | Oshawa Creek, Wilmot Creek, Bowmanville Creek, Cobourg Creek, Soper Creek, Graham Creek, Shelter Valley Creek | East Lake Ontario; lower pressure than west-end rivers | No | Low–Med |
| ⭐ | Twenty Mile Creek, Niagara River | Niagara Peninsula; lower pressure; Twenty Mile open all year lower section | No | Low |
| ⭐ | Mad River, Sydenham River, Sauble River | Georgian Bay; consistent fall steelhead | No | Low |
| ⭐ | **Big Creek**, Big Otter Creek, Lynn River, Nanticoke Creek | Lake Erie tribs — **steelhead only, no Chinook**; Big Creek is the best of these | No | Low |

## Key Dates — 2026

| Date | Event |
|---|---|

### Southwest — Lake Erie Tributaries

> These rivers are **farther from Toronto** (1h 30 min to 2h 30 min). They are **warmer, lower-gradient agricultural streams** compared to Lake Ontario tribs. Steelhead (rainbow trout) are the primary migratory target; Chinook salmon are essentially absent. **Big Creek** is the standout; the others are secondary options.
>
> All Lake Erie tributaries listed here are in **FMZ 16**. Conservation Authorities: LPRCA (Lynn River, Nanticoke Creek, Big Creek, Big Otter Creek), KCCA (Kettle Creek), CCCA (Catfish Creek).
>
> **FMZ 16 default season:** 4th Saturday in April to September 30. Extended fall season on named tributaries below: 4th Saturday in April to **December 31**. Some sections: **open all year**. Zone-wide limit Oct 1–Dec 31 drops to S-1, C-0 except on extended-season waters.

## Crowd Levels

> Ratings reflect **peak season pressure** (spring steelhead opener, fall run). Off-peak or mid-week these drop a tier. C&R-only rules and artificial-only restrictions naturally thin crowds.

| Pressure | Rivers | Why / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **High** | Ganaraska River | Highest steelhead numbers in the province = everyone knows it; April opener is shoulder to shoulder at the popular holes |
| **High** | Credit River (lower — Erindale, Streetsville) | Urban, easy parking, well-documented online; very busy on weekends during runs |
| **High** | Humber River (lower) | Urban, easy access from Toronto; crowded spring and fall |
| **High** | Grand River — Elora Gorge | Iconic fly fishing destination; famous enough to draw anglers from far away; busy weekends all season |
| **Medium** | Don River, Rouge River | Urban; spread out enough that it's not always bad; more pressure near easy parking |
| **Medium** | Bronte Creek, 16 Mile Creek | West Lake Ontario tribs; popular but not Ganaraska-level |
| **Medium** | Duffins Creek | Well-known east trib; busy at Greenwood CA during runs |
| **Medium** | Grand River — Fergus (Anderson St / Templin) | Busy but walkable stretches spread anglers out; better than Elora Gorge |
| **Medium** | Nottawasaga River (near Alliston / highway bridges) | Known steelhead river; popular access spots get crowded; walk in from road bridges for space |
| **Medium** | Saugeen River (Southampton mouth) | Mouth gets crowded; walk upriver or fish Denny's Dam area for more room |
| **Medium** | Oshawa Creek, Bowmanville Creek | East Lake Ontario; moderate pressure during runs; less than Credit/Humber |
| **Low** | Upper Credit (Forks, Cataract, Inglewood) | C&R only + artificial lures filters out most bait anglers; small stream = self-limiting; generally quiet |
| **Low** | West Credit River, Shaw's Creek | C&R + artificial; niche fly/spin audience; low traffic |
| **Low** | East Humber River | Spring-fed, less-known; Boyd CA and Bindertwine Park access see low pressure |
| **Low** | Eramosa River, Speed River | Landlocked — no steelhead/salmon run means no run-chasing crowds; resident trout only |
| **Low** | Innisfil Creek | Rarely mentioned online; mostly local knowledge |
| **Low** | Mad River, Pine River, Noisy River | Georgian Bay area; long drive filters out casual anglers; good stretches to yourself |
| **Low** | Beaver River, Bighead River | Niagara Escarpment tribs; beautiful water, low pressure; Thornbury is known but walkable |
| **Low** | Sydenham River, Sauble River, Maitland River | Georgian Bay / Lake Huron tribs; 2h+ drive; far enough to thin the crowds significantly |
| **Low** | Big Creek, Big Otter Creek, Lynn River, Nanticoke Creek | Lake Erie tribs; 1h45–2h15 drive + steelhead-only (no salmon) keeps most Toronto anglers away |
| **Low** | Wilmot Creek, Cobourg Creek, Shelter Valley Creek, Soper Creek, Graham Creek | East Lake Ontario; 55–90 min drive; well east of the main crowd; low documented pressure |
| **Low** | Etobicoke Creek | Small urban creek; not worth the crowd it does get; low value |
| **Very Low** | Niagara River gorge | Complex access (steep terrain, border regulations); self-selects for serious anglers only |

### Bottom Line

If you're doing a **2h+ drive, go Low pressure** — you're earning elbow room and it should pay off. The High/Medium rivers are for nearby weekday trips or when you want a proven run with easy access. The sweet spot for a full day trip is **Beaver/Bighead, Mad/Pine/Noisy, Big Creek, or the Saugeen** (away from the mouth).

## Licensing & Regulations

- **Ontario Outdoors Card + Fishing Licence** required — buy at ServiceOntario or online
- Check **Fish ON-Line** app for species-specific regulations by waterbody
- Many rivers near Toronto have **special catch-and-release only zones** — always verify
- Some conservation area access requires a **day-use fee**
- Ganaraska and Credit River have specific slot/size limits — check annually
