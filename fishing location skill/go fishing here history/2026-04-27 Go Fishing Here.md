### Best Day: Sunday May 3, 2026

**Top pick river:** Wilmot Creek (mouth/lower at Newcastle, plus runs upstream now that full system is open)
- Why: Bigger basin = more water and holding pools than Graham. Apr 28–29 rain was small — not enough to blow out, just enough to bump flows and trigger fish movement. By Sun (5 days post-peak) clarity is fully recovered. Sun is the warmest day (13°C) → most active fish. Spring run is winding down, but drop-back steelhead and residents are on the move; full system open since Apr 25 means you can hike upstream away from the mouth crowd.
- Access (multi-spot — work upstream as pressure dictates):
  - **Samuel Wilmot Nature Area** (mouth, primary) — Cobbledick Rd south of Hwy 2; two parking lots (use east lot if west closed); footbridge over creek — [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Samuel+Wilmot+Nature+Area+Newcastle+Ontario)

### What to Expect

- **Species:** Drop-back steelhead (heading lake-bound), resident rainbow trout, brown trout, suckers. Spring run winding down — fewer fresh fish, but drop-backs are aggressive and hungry.
- **Water temps:** Cool (8–11°C lower sections). Slow your retrieve; fish hold deeper in pools.
- **Tactics:** Float fishing with single-egg rigs (chartreuse, pink, white), small spoons (Cleos, Little Cleos) and spinners (size 2–3) near the mouth. Above CNR on Wilmot — try small streamers, Panther Martins, or float with worms in deeper bends.
- **Gear:** 8'6"–10'6" medium light steelhead rod, 6lb mono or 8lb braid + 6lb fluoro leader. Waders not strictly needed in lower sections but useful in upper Wilmot.
- **Cautions:** Cold overnight lows mean ice on guides if going at first light. Wilmot mouth gets pressure on weekends — go early or hike upstream. Graham is virtually unfished but very small; bring stealth.
- **Regulations:** FMZ 17, full open since Apr 25. Lower sections C&R or 1 fish (check 2026 regs); upper inland sections standard limits.
