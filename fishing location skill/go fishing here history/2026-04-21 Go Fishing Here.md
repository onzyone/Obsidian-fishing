### What to Expect

**Species:** Late steelhead holdovers in the lower reaches, staging lake-run brown trout at creek mouths, early resident browns as water hits 10–12°C midday. No confirmed run push yet — prospect-fish, don't expect stacks.

**Tactics for clear water (Thu Apr 23):**
- Float: small roe bags (natural/peach), worms, small jigs in muted colours (black, brown, olive). Downsize leaders — 6–8lb fluoro.
- Fly: egg patterns muted (sucker spawn, apricot), small stoneflies, hare's ear nymphs under indicator. Small olive/black buggers in pools.
- Spin: 1/8 oz spoons, small spinners, light hardware.

**Timing:** Mid-morning to early afternoon (09:00–14:00) — overnight low 4°C means water is cold at first light; wait for sun to bump water a degree or two.

**Gear:** Full waders — 6–10°C water, too cold to wet-wade. Polarized glasses essential for sight-fishing clear water at mouths.

**Sanity checks Thursday AM:**
- Credit gauge: [WSC 02HB029](https://wateroffice.ec.gc.ca/report/real_time_e.html?stn=02HB029) — primary regional benchmark for all West creeks
- Humber gauge: [WSC 02HC003](https://wateroffice.ec.gc.ca/report/real_time_e.html?stn=02HC003) — cross-check
- CRAA Credit cam: [craa.on.ca/live-camera-river-watcher](https://www.craa.on.ca/live-camera-river-watcher/)

If Credit shows any sharp overnight spike, pivot to Etobicoke (smallest watershed = fastest recovery) or fish higher Credit near Erindale above most tributary inputs.
