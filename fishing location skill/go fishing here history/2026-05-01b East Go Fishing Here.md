### What to Expect

- **Species:** Drop-back steelhead at lake mouths and below dams/ladders; resident rainbow/brown trout; suckers stacking. Bowmanville also gets some chinook holdover.
- **Bowmanville tactics:** Float fishing single-egg bags (chartreuse, pink, white) or roe sacs through Spry Ave runs. Small spoons (Cleos #2, Krocodiles silver/blue or fire tiger) and inline spinners (Mepps #2, Blue Fox) in deeper holes. Hit prime zone at first light, expand to King St / Roenigk after 8am.
- **Wilmot/Duffins tactics:** Float roe at mouths for staging fish; below Greenwood dam = float through the deep run.
- **Gear:** 8'6"–10'6" ML steelhead rod, centerpin or spinning reel, 6lb mono mainline (or 8lb braid), 6lb fluoro leader.
- **Cautions:**
  - **Cold morning Sat (0.5°C)** — guides will freeze. De-icer or thermos with hot water; fingerless wool gloves.
  - **Full moon Sat overnight feed** — softer dawn bite likely; mid-morning push (8–10am).
  - **Sun drizzle** — fishable but bring rain shell; light stain often helps bite.
  - **Sanctuaries** — Bowmanville fish ladder, Ganaraska Hwy 401→Jocelyn St Bridge.
- **Regulations:** Always verify on [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary). FMZ 17 lower sections all open since Apr 25.
