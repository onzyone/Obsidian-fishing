### What to Expect

- **Species:** Drop-back steelhead (lake-bound, aggressive at the harbour mouth on Twenty Mile and Soper), resident rainbow/brown trout, suckers, and on Twenty Mile some northern pike + rock bass in lower flats.
- **Twenty Mile tactics:** Float with single-egg bags (chartreuse / pink / white), small spoons (Little Cleos #2, Krocodiles), inline spinners (Mepps #2). Work the harbour mouth at first light — drop-backs stage there. Move to the QEW bridge if the mouth gets crowded. Lower river is wider and slower than typical GTA tribs.
- **Soper tactics:** Hike from Simpson Ave or Liberty St into the trail corridor; fish bend pools and tail-outs of riffles. Mouth at Bowmanville Waterfront if you want lake-staging fish.
- **Gear:** 8'6"–10'6" ML steelhead rod, 6lb mono mainline or 8lb braid, 6lb fluoro leader. Waders helpful but not strictly required for the lake-mouth approach on Twenty Mile.
- **Cautions:** Cold overnight lows (1–2°C) — ice on guides at first light; bring de-icer or thermos. Full moon = potential softer dawn; have a mid-morning plan B (8–10am push). Twenty Mile harbour mouth is exposed — check wind forecast Sat morning before the drive. Soper has a fish-ladder sanctuary lower-mid — verify boundary signage onsite.
- **Regulations:** Twenty Mile lower (QEW → Lake Ontario) — FMZ 16, no close season, S-5 / C-2 rainbow/brown trout. Soper lower — year-round (verify annually); upper FMZ 17 since Apr 25. Always verify on [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary).
