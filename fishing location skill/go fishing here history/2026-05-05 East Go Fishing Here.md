### What to Expect

- **Species:** Lingering steelhead drop-backs at lake mouths, resident rainbow / brown trout, suckers stacking. Bowmanville may hold late chinook.
- **Sunday tactics (post-storm slight stain):**
  - **Wilmot/Oshawa mouths:** float roe sacs (chartreuse, pink) at first light through staging fish; small spoons (Cleos #2, Krocodiles silver/blue or fire tiger) when light is up.
  - **Bowmanville Spry Ave:** float single-egg bags or roe through deeper runs; switch to inline spinners (Mepps #2, Blue Fox) once sun on water.
- **Gear:** 8'6"–10'6" ML steelhead rod, centerpin or spinning reel, 6lb mono mainline (or 8lb braid), 6lb fluoro leader. Bring rain shell + extra socks (post-storm = wet banks, slick rocks).
- **Cautions:**
  - **Saturday is a hard SKIP** — Fri 12mm + Sat 8mm = peak storm. Do not drive Sat.
  - **Pre-trip gauge check Saturday night** — refresh 02HD009 (Wilmot), 02HD008 (Oshawa), 02HD006 (Bowmanville). Want to see Sat-night peak then dropping. If still rising, push entire trip.
  - **Last Quarter moon (~36–46%)** = balanced; standard dawn-patrol applies.
  - **Sanctuaries** — Bowmanville fish ladder (Roenigk lot fish *below* it), Ganaraska Hwy 401→Jocelyn St Bridge.
