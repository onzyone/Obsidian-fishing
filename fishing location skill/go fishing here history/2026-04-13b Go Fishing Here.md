### Weather & River Summary

A heavy multi-day storm hits Apr 13–16 (~53mm total: 2.8mm Mon, 19.4mm Tue, 27mm Wed, 3.9mm Thu), which will blow out all West and East rivers by mid-week. Friday Apr 17 is the first fully dry day (0mm, 17.8°C, mostly clear) but rivers will still be near peak on Day 1 of recovery — excellent weather, risky water. Saturday Apr 18 (19.6°C, 1.8mm drizzle) is Day 2 of recovery — medium rivers dropping hard with fish actively moving; mild temperatures make it the most comfortable option. Sunday Apr 19 (12.5°C, 0.3mm, **near-freezing overnight at 0.1°C**) is Day 3 — best clarity, worst weather. Both gauged rivers (Credit and Humber) are currently falling and fishable before the storm hits, with clarity inferred as clearing.

### Best Day: Saturday April 18

**Top pick river:** Credit River lower (Erindale Park)
- Why: Day 2 from a 53mm storm — river dropping fast from a high pulse. Steelhead stack in deep pools and feeding lies on falling water; this is the most active window. Best stocked West river (1,200 rainbow trout) with early run emergence confirmed. Mild (19.6°C), minimal rain (1.8mm drizzle), year-round lower section open.
- Access: Erindale Park, Dundas St W, Mississauga — free, large lot — [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Erindale+Park+Mississauga)
- Drive: ~30–40 min from 427 & Finch

**Backup:** Duffins Creek lower (Greenwood CA, Pickering)
- Why: Similar recovery timeline to Humber/Credit, less weekend pressure than Credit. Medium river with 400 stocked brown trout and pre-peak steelhead.
- Access: Greenwood Conservation Area, Greenwood Rd, Pickering — day-use fee — [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Greenwood+Conservation+Area+Pickering)
- Drive: ~45–50 min from 427 & Finch

### What to Expect

**Saturday conditions:** Water temp likely 6–9°C post-storm. Slightly coloured to murky water — use bright attractor patterns: pink/chartreuse egg beads, spawn bags, orange jigs, or bright spoons. Float fishing or nymphing in deep pools and current seams. Fish are actively moving and feeding on dropping water. Arrive early — Saturday pressure on Credit is high.
