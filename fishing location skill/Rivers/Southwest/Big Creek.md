# Big Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 45 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), brown trout (resident), brook trout (upper sections)
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May) and Fall (Oct–Dec) for steelhead; brown trout year-round
**Conservation Authority:** Long Point Region CA (LPRCA) — 519-842-4242
**FMZ:** 16 (southwestern Ontario)
**NWA Contact:** 1-800-668-6767 / Enviroinfo@ec.gc.ca

---

## Overview

Consistently rated the **best Lake Erie tributary in Ontario for steelhead**. Flows ~64 km south through Norfolk County from Windham Township to Long Point Bay. The lower section is federally protected as **Big Creek National Wildlife Area (NWA)**. Receives far less pressure than Lake Ontario tribs — the 1h 45 min drive filters out most Toronto-area anglers.

No Chinook salmon — Lake Erie essentially has none. Steelhead strain is the Ganaraska/Normandale strain, well-adapted to Great Lakes tributaries. A fishway at Quance Dam has opened upstream habitat and improved run quality over the past decade.

## Regulations — FMZ 16

> ⚠️ Always verify current variation orders at [ontario.ca FMZ 16](https://www.ontario.ca/page/sport-fishing-variation-order-fisheries-management-zone-16)

| Section | Open Season | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **Below Regional Rd 21 → Long Point Bay** | All year (no close time) | S-5, C-2 |
| **Quance Dam → Regional Rd 21 (Lynedoch)** | 4th Sat April – Dec 31 | S-1 Oct–Dec (zone-wide variation) |
| **Teeterville Dam → Quance Dam** | 4th Sat April – Sept 30 ONLY | S-5 (Apr–Sept); **closed Oct–Apr** |

**Key implication:** The upper section (Teeterville–Quance) is closed for the entire fall steelhead season. Focus fall/spring steelhead effort on the lower two sections only.

## Sections — Where to Fish

### Lower Section (Regional Rd 21 / Lynedoch → Long Point Bay)
- **Prime steelhead water.** Open all year.
- Fish push in from Long Point Bay in fall (Oct–Dec) and spring (Mar–May).
- Section near and below the Hwy 59 crossing (NWA) is most accessible.
- Brown trout hold in deeper pools and log jams year-round.
- Water becomes marshier approaching Long Point Bay.

### Mid Section (Quance Dam → Regional Rd 21)
- Good holding water for fish that have passed the Quance Dam fishway.
- Deep green pools under fallen logs are key holding spots.
- **Below Quance Dam pool:** Fish stack here before ascending the fishway — classic choke point.
- **Dick's Hill Bridge:** Named steel bridge, loose oak timbers. Road-allowance fishing access.
- Most riparian land is private farmland — access is at road bridge crossings only.

### Upper Section (Teeterville Dam → Quance Dam)
- Closed Oct–Apr. Summer only: brook and small brown trout.
- Shallower character, less relevant for steelhead.

## Public vs. Private Land

**Public access:**
- Big Creek NWA (770 ha federally managed) — both units open to public sport fishing
- Rowan Mills CA and Lower Big Creek CA (LPRCA) — free public access
- All road bridge crossings — road allowance fishing permitted

**Private land warning:**
Ontario has **no high-water rule**. Creek bed and banks between road crossings are private property. Big Creek runs through intensively farmed agricultural land for most of its length. Wading up/downstream from a bridge through adjacent farmland without permission is trespass.

Canoe/kayak from Rowan Mills CA or Port Rowan boat launch is the best way to reach mid-creek water otherwise inaccessible on foot.

## Seasonal Access Warnings

- **NWA trail closure (Hwy 59):** Closed during fall bird migration — overlaps with peak steelhead season (Oct–Dec). Confirm dates before going: 1-800-668-6767.
- **Waterfowl hunting in NWA:** Sept 10 – Dec 20. Wear blaze orange if fishing NWA land during this window.

## Run Timing

| Period | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Oct – Dec (peak fall)** | Water cools below 55°F; fish push in from Long Point Bay. Best after rain raises and colours water then falls/clears. Large, strong fish after summer lake feeding. |
| **Mar – May (spring)** | Good fishing, especially March. Fish that wintered in creek move upstream to spawn. |
| **Year-round** | Resident brown trout in deeper pools and log jams. |

---

## Wading & Conditions

- **Bottom:** Sandy/muddy with significant log hazards. Studs or aggressive rubber soles recommended.
- **Clarity:** Agricultural watershed — blows out fast after rain. Best 1–2 days after rainfall on falling, clearing flows.
- **Current:** Low gradient, generally moderate. Shallow riffles to deep murky green pools — wade carefully at new spots.
- **Summer:** Warms quickly due to low gradient and agricultural drainage. Not productive for steelhead Jul–Aug.
