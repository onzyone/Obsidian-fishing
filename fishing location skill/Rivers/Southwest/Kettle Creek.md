# Kettle Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~2h
**Species:** Warm-water fish (carp, catfish, bass); rainbow trout in upper tributaries only
**Seasons:** Spring only (trout); warm-water species year-round
**Conservation Authority:** Kettle Creek Conservation Authority (KCCA)
**FMZ:** 16 (southwestern Ontario)

---

## Overview

> ⚠️ **Not a steelhead river.** Kettle Creek is a warm-water dominated watershed. Do not make the drive expecting steelhead — this is not the same fishery as Big Creek or Big Otter Creek.

Flows through Elgin County to Lake Erie at Port Stanley. KCCA wildlife surveys confirm rainbow trout only in cooler upper tributaries (Little Creek, Mill Creek) — not in the main stem. The Port Stanley harbour mouth may see a handful of steelhead in early spring but is not a reliable or productive target.

---

## Regulations — FMZ 16

> ⚠️ Always verify at [ontario.ca FMZ 16](https://www.ontario.ca/page/sport-fishing-variation-order-fisheries-management-zone-16)

| Section | Open Season | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Main stem / lower creek** | Verify in current regs | Likely standard FMZ 16 (4th Sat April – Sept 30 or Dec 31) |
| **Upper tributaries (Little Creek, Mill Creek)** | 4th Sat April – Sept 30 | Cooler water, small resident rainbow trout |

Confirm specific sections in current regs before targeting trout.

## Practical Notes

- Drive time (~2h) is not justified for trout — Big Creek or Big Otter Creek are both better options at similar distance
- Worth knowing about for warm-water fishing (bass, carp) while in the Port Stanley area
- Contact KCCA for current access information
