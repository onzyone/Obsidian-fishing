# Nanticoke Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 30–1h 45 min
**Species:** Steelhead, brown trout
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Dec)
**Conservation Authority:** Long Point Region CA (LPRCA) — 519-842-4242
**FMZ:** 16 (southwestern Ontario)

---

## Overview

Small Lake Erie tributary draining into Lake Erie near Nanticoke (Haldimand County). Lightly fished and not well-documented in angling media. Part of the LPRCA Nanticoke/Sandusk/Stoney Creek subwatershed. Steelhead and brown trout are possible but this is a secondary option relative to Big Creek.

---

## Regulations — FMZ 16

> ⚠️ Always verify at [ontario.ca FMZ 16](https://www.ontario.ca/page/sport-fishing-variation-order-fisheries-management-zone-16)

| Section | Open Season | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Named sections** | 4th Sat April – Dec 31 | Extended fall season likely applies |
| **Upper / unlisted sections** | 4th Sat April – Sept 30 | Standard FMZ 16 default |

No documented special C&R rules. Standard limit S-5, C-2; S-1, C-0 Oct–Dec outside extended-season sections.

## Practical Notes

- Limited public access information available — do due diligence before visiting
- Small creek, agricultural watershed — runs turbid after rain
- Very low fishing pressure — may hold steelhead with little competition
- Big Creek is a more reliable and better-documented option nearby
