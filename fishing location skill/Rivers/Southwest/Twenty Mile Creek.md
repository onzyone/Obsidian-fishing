# Twenty Mile Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~55–65 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), Chinook salmon, brown trout, northern pike, rock bass
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Nov)
**Conservation Authority:** Niagara Peninsula Conservation Authority (NPCA)
**FMZ:** 16 (southwestern Ontario)

---

## Overview

Lake Ontario tributary (not Lake Erie) flowing into Jordan Harbour near Jordan, Niagara Peninsula. Confirmed steelhead runs with significantly lower pressure than Credit River or Humber. Chinook salmon possible in fall. A good lower-pressure alternative within reasonable drive time.

---

## Regulations — FMZ 16

> ⚠️ Always verify at [ontario.ca FMZ 16](https://www.ontario.ca/page/sport-fishing-variation-order-fisheries-management-zone-16)

| Section | Open Season | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **QEW to Lake Ontario (lower)** | All year (no close time) | S-5, C-2 for rainbow and brown trout |
| **Above QEW** | 4th Sat April – Sept 30 (standard) | Standard FMZ 16 |

---

## Access Points

| Location | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Jordan Harbour Conservation Area** | Main access point. Parking off Lakeshore Rd (Regional Rd 87). Walk to creek mouth and lower section. |
| **Lakeshore Rd (Regional Rd 87) crossing** | Road-allowance access near harbour. |
| **QEW bridge crossing** | Road allowance; lower river access. |

---

## Practical Notes

- Significantly lower pressure than Credit, Humber, or Duffins
- Lower section open all year — viable winter option for steelhead
- Steelhead confirmed on Fishbrain and NPCA watershed data
- Agricultural and suburban watershed — clarity varies; best on clearing flows after rain
