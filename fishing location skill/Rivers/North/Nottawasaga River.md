# Nottawasaga River ("The Notty")

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 10–20 min (via Hwy 400 N)
**Species:** Wild steelhead, Chinook salmon; known for the best-fighting steelhead in Ontario
**Seasons:** Early Sept – end of May; peak: mid-Oct to late Dec, and late Apr – early May

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|
| Georgian Bay → Boyne R. confluence | No | Year-round |
| **Boyne R. → Pine R. confluence** | ✅ **C&R only (rainbow trout)** | Year-round · **CLOSED** Oct 1 – Dec 31 (sanctuary) |
| Above Boyne R. | No | Apr 25 – Sept 30 |

## Notes

- Largest wild steelhead and salmon river flowing into **Georgian Bay**
- These wild Nottawasaga steelhead are renowned for their fight — genetic strain never stocked
- **Challenge:** much of the river is private property — public access sections are limited
- Accessible public water in middle/lower sections, but water can be slow, flat, muddy
- Worth the effort if you can find public access — fish quality is exceptional
- Tributaries: Mad River, Boyne River, Pine River, Noisy River, Willow Creek
- **Wasaga Beach mouth** is the single best public access point — wide flat water, mallards often congregate here (open water in winter = fish holding)
- Murky greenish-brown water is normal and consistent — don't be deterred; fish use the turbidity as cover
- Wide flat inside bends with gravel/silt bars are key holding features — classic lower-reach morphology
- **Special no-kill zone: Angus to Alliston section** — all fish must be released in this stretch; verify exact boundaries in current regs
- Winter steelhead: lower river near Wasaga Beach stays open year-round — ducks on the ice shelf mark the open-water holes where fish hold

## Access Points

> Most of the river downstream of Alliston is private property. Public access is limited to the locations below. Contact NVCA for current maps.

| Location | Notes | Maps |
|---|---|---|
| Wasaga Beach (river mouth) | **Best public walk-up access.** Parking near beach/river mouth; wide flat water; fish hold here year-round in open-water sections in winter | [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Wasaga+Beach+Nottawasaga+River+mouth) |
| Edenvale Conservation Area | NVCA-managed; picnic area; good mid-river access to the Nottawasaga | [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Edenvale+Conservation+Area+Ontario+Nottawasaga) |
| Angus — Alma Street field | Field access across from Alma St; can drive to within ~100m of river; some public sections near town | [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Alma+Street+Angus+Ontario+Nottawasaga+River) |
