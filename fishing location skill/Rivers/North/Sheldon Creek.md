# Sheldon Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 15–25 min (Loretto / Adjala-Tosorontio area)
**Species:** Brook trout (upper — Escarpment spring source), brown trout, steelhead/rainbow trout (spawning migrants from Nottawasaga), salmon (spawning); warmwater species (bass, pike, rock bass) in lower slower reaches near Nottawasaga confluence
**Seasons:** Standard FMZ 16 season

## Notes

- **One of the coldest, cleanest, and healthiest streams in the Nottawasaga Watershed**
- Flows out of the **Niagara Escarpment**, providing high-quality coldwater trout habitat and excellent spawning grounds for migratory trout and salmon pushing up from the Nottawasaga
- Part of NVCA's **Nottawasaga River Restoration Program** — targeted for water quality improvement and habitat restoration
- Home to native brook trout, river burbot, with species at risk including northern brook lamprey
- Water quality and trout habitat degrade moving downstream through Adjala-Tosorontio due to lack of riparian forest cover — summer temperatures rise in lower reaches
- Best opportunities are **upper reaches closer to the Escarpment** where cold water keeps brook trout present year-round
- Low pressure — rarely mentioned as a destination; known mostly to locals and NVCA volunteers
- Small stream — light tackle, tight cover, careful approach

## Access Points

> ⚠️ Sheldon Creek access is limited. Much of the creek flows through **private agricultural land** in Adjala-Tosorontio. Public access is concentrated at road crossings and NVCA holdings. Always ask permission when in doubt, and never park blocking farm gates.
