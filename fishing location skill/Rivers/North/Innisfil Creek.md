# Innisfil Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~50–60 min (Hwy 400 N to Cookstown)
**Species:** Brown trout (resident), brook trout, steelhead (lower sections near Nottawasaga confluence)
**Seasons:** Spring (Apr–Jun best for residents); Fall (Oct–Nov steelhead staging near mouth)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Innisfil Creek (general) | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Sept 30 (FMZ 16) |
| Near Nottawasaga confluence | No | Standard | Verify — Nottawasaga sanctuary rules may extend to mouth |

- **FMZ 16** — standard zone-wide trout season: 4th Saturday in April to September 30
- Lower sections near the Nottawasaga confluence may be subject to the Boyne–Pine sanctuary zone rules Oct 1 – Dec 31 — **verify current regs**
- Tributary of the Nottawasaga River; drains north through Cookstown into the Nottawasaga system

## Notes

- Small-to-medium freestone stream running through Cookstown (Town of Innisfil), Simcoe County
- **Fish ladder — UNCONFIRMED** — no public records, NVCA documents, or fishing forums confirm a fish ladder exists on Innisfil Creek in Cookstown; needs verification before making a trip
- Brown trout are the primary resident species; brook trout possible in cooler upper sections
- Steelhead from Georgian Bay may push into lower Innisfil Creek via the Nottawasaga in spring and fall
- Lower pressure than the main Nottawasaga; worth scouting in early spring when main river is blown out
- Water can warm quickly in summer — best fished spring through early June and again in fall
- Managed under **Nottawasaga Valley Conservation Authority (NVCA)**
