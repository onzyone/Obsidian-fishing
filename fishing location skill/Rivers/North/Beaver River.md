# Beaver River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 40–50 min
**Species:** Wild steelhead (rainbow trout), brown trout, Chinook salmon (limited)
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Nov)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower section (mouth/harbour area) | No | Standard | Year-round (FMZ 16) |
| Upper sections | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Sept 30 (FMZ 16) |

- Standard limits apply on all open sections
- Verify current regs annually — lower section year-round designation should be confirmed

## Notes

- Flows into Nottawasaga Bay (Georgian Bay) near Thornbury/Meaford
- One of the best wild steelhead rivers on Georgian Bay — cold, clear water draining the Beaver Valley
- The river cuts through the Niagara Escarpment, creating excellent holding water with pools and riffles
- Lower flows in summer; best fished in spring and fall when water is up
- **Thornbury fish ladder** on the lower river is a key landmark and access point — large numbers of steelhead stage here in spring and fall
- More scenic and cleaner water than the Nottawasaga River
- Better public access than the Notty, though some private land issues remain
- Blue Mountains ski area is nearby — can combine a trip with other activities
