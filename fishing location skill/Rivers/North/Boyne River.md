# Boyne River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 20–30 min
**Species:** Wild brown trout, brook trout, steelhead (fall — near Nottawasaga confluence)
**Seasons:** Spring/Summer for resident trout; Fall for steelhead near the Nottawasaga confluence

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boyne River (resident trout sections) | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Sept 30 (FMZ 16) |
| **Boyne R. → Pine R. section of Nottawasaga** | ✅ **C&R only (rainbow trout)** | Standard | Year-round · **CLOSED** Oct 1 – Dec 31 (sanctuary) |

- Standard limits apply on Boyne River open sections (resident trout)
- The Boyne R. mouth defines the key regulatory boundary on the Nottawasaga River
- **The Boyne–Pine section of the Nottawasaga is a C&R sanctuary Oct 1 – Dec 31** — verify exact boundaries annually

## Notes

- Primary tributary of the Nottawasaga River, joining near Alliston
- The Boyne confluence defines the critical regulatory boundary on the Nottawasaga — C&R zone starts at the Boyne mouth
- Upper reaches (Shelburne/Orangeville area headwaters) hold wild brown trout and brook trout in cold, spring-fed water
- Lower Boyne near Alliston becomes a staging area for steelhead before they push into the Nottawasaga system in fall
- **The Boyne meets the Nottawasaga near Alliston** — key access corridor for the Notty's regulated zone
- Private land is a significant challenge throughout the watershed
- Some road allowance access near Shelburne and Orangeville for upper sections
