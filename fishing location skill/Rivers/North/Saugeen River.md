# Saugeen River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~2h 15–30 min
**Species:** Wild steelhead (rainbow trout), Chinook salmon, brown trout, resident brook trout (upper sections)
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May) and Fall (Oct–Dec); some year-round sections

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower section (below Denny's Dam / Walkerton) | No | Standard | Extended fall or year-round — verify FMZ 23 |
| Upper sections | No | Standard | Standard season (FMZ 23) |
| Below Denny's Dam (Walkerton) | No | Standard | Key staging area — verify current season dates |

- Standard limits apply on all open sections
- **Denny's Dam in Walkerton is a key steelhead barrier** — large numbers of fish stage below it
- Verify lower section extended/year-round designation in current FMZ 23 regulations annually

## Notes

- One of Ontario's premier steelhead rivers — the Saugeen is to Lake Huron what the Ganaraska is to Lake Ontario
- Flows into Lake Huron near Southampton; large watershed with excellent water volume
- **Southampton/Port Elgin area** at the mouth is the prime steelhead access zone
- Chinook salmon run also strong in fall
- Wild fish — not heavily stocked; Saugeen Salmon Association runs the hatchery and has local knowledge
- **Denny's Dam near Walkerton** is a major staging area — fish queue up below the dam before dispersing upstream
- Bruce Peninsula scenery; a destination river worth the 2.5h drive for trophy steelhead

## Denny's Dam — Spot Notes

Denny's Dam is the most productive single location on the Saugeen for migratory fish. The dam sits in the hamlet of the same name, ~10 km south of Hanover on County Rd 3 (not Walkerton — the dam is upstream of Walkerton). Fish stage in the tailout and pool directly below the dam waiting for higher water to move over or around it.

**Why it fishes so well:**
- The dam is a partial barrier — fish don't easily pass until water levels are right, so they congregate
- Deep scour pool directly below holds large numbers of fish during peak run
- Both banks accessible; Crown land on river corridor

**When to go:**
- Spring steelhead: mid-March through April — fish stack below dam as run builds
- Fall steelhead: October through November
- Best after a rain event when river is **dropping and clearing** — fish are actively moving
- Water temp trigger: fish move aggressively once temps hit 5–8°C in spring

**Tactics:**
- Nymphing with egg patterns or stoneflies in the tailout below the dam
- Swinging streamers (white, olive, pink) through the deeper pool
- Float fishing with spawn sacs or beads effective in the holding pool
- Can be crowded on weekends during peak run — arrive early
