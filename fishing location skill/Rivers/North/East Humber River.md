# East Humber River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~30–45 min (Boyd CA / Bindertwine Park area)
**Species:** Brook trout (resident, upper), brown trout (resident), steelhead/rainbow trout (seasonal, lower), salmon (seasonal, lower)
**Seasons:** Spring (Apr 25 – May) for migratory species; year-round resident brook and brown trout (when open)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower East Humber (to main Humber confluence) | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Sept 30 (FMZ 16) |
| Upper East Humber (above Boyd CA) | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Sept 30 (FMZ 16) |

- Steelhead and salmon access limited by the seasonal opening (Apr 25) — no year-round migratory fishing above the lower Humber
- Brook trout present in upper reaches year-round but only legally fishable during open season
- No sanctuary or C&R zone specific to East Humber — standard FMZ 16 rules apply
- **Always verify** — the Humber system has complex sanctuary rules above Hwy 407 on the main branch; confirm East Humber section rules annually

## Why Fish This Over the Main Humber

The East Humber originates on the **Oak Ridges Moraine** — a massive glacial ridge that acts as a groundwater reservoir. The headwater tributaries are spring-fed and cold even in summer. This means:

- **Clears faster** than the main Humber after rain (smaller, moraine-fed watershed)
- **Brook trout** in the upper sections — a species the lower main Humber doesn't have
- **Much less pressure** — most anglers go straight to the lower Humber; the East branch feels like a wilderness river further upstream
- Good clear-water fallback when the main Humber is blown out and murky

## Notes

- Smaller river than the main Humber branch — tight casting in upper sections; 5x–6x tippet, small flies
- Further upstream from Boyd CA it genuinely feels remote despite being in York Region
- MNR Atlas (2003) confirms brook trout in the upper portion (watershed 2HC-04, Vaughan Township)
- Sources: NRVIS, HABPROG, Barton et al. (1985)
- Barrier mitigation projects on the Humber system have improved steelhead/salmon access to some tributaries — check current TRCA reports for updates

## Run Timing

- **Brook trout:** Resident; best April–June (post-spawn feeding) and September–October (pre-winter)
- **Brown trout:** Resident in mid-upper sections; best spring and fall
- **Steelhead/salmon:** Seasonal only (Apr 25 open); early spring push if water temps allow
