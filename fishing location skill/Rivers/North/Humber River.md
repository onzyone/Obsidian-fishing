# Humber River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~10–20 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), migratory brown trout, Chinook salmon, bass, pike
**Seasons:** Late Feb – mid-May (steelhead spring run); Sept – Dec (salmon & fall steelhead); year-round lower river

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

## Notes

- Enters Lake Ontario ~12 km from downtown Toronto — closest migratory trout river
- Lower Humber (Eglinton Ave to Lake Ontario) open year-round
- Steelhead enter late February as ice breaks, fish until mid-May
- Wild fish that fight harder than stocked rivers
- **West Humber River** (tributary) enters the main Humber at Summerlea Park — confluence creates a holding pool; worth fishing when fish are staging
- **East Humber River** (tributary) — see [[East Humber River]] for full details; brook trout in upper sections, Boyd CA and Bindertwine Park access

| Location | Maps |

| Palgrave Forest and Wildlife Area — headwaters; brook trout; walk-in | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Palgrave+Forest+and+Wildlife+Area+Ontario) |
| Glen Haffy Conservation Area — headwater brookies (FMZ 17 inland) | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Glen+Haffy+Conservation+Area+Caledon+Ontario) |

> Upper Humber (Bolton–Palgrave) holds resident brown trout and brook trout — no stocking program currently active; wild fish only. Cold Creek (tributary near Bolton) is confirmed brook trout water. C&R strongly encouraged on this stretch. Season: Apr 25 – Sept 30 (FMZ 16).
>
> ⚠️ **Sinkhole warning:** The Upper Humber riverbed has sinkholes just below the gravel surface — soft, whitish-grey clay. Anglers can get stuck. Probe with a wading staff before committing weight and avoid areas where the bottom looks pale or soft.
