# Sydenham River (Georgian Bay)

> **Note:** This is the Sydenham River near Owen Sound / Georgian Bay — not to be confused with the Sydenham River near Chatham in the Lake St. Clair watershed.

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~2h – 2h 15 min
**Species:** Wild steelhead (rainbow trout), brown trout, brook trout (upper reaches)
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Nov)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower section (Owen Sound mouth area) | No | Standard | Year-round (verify — FMZ 16) |
| Upper sections | No | Standard | Standard season (FMZ 16) |

- Standard limits apply on all open sections
- Verify lower section year-round designation in current FMZ 16 regulations

## Notes

- Flows into Owen Sound Bay (Georgian Bay) at Owen Sound
- Part of the Georgian Bay trib cluster alongside the Beaver and Bighead rivers
- Known for cold, clear water and scenic Niagara Escarpment setting
- Upper reaches in the Grey Highlands hold brook trout
- The Owen Sound mouth area and Harrison Park (in Owen Sound) are key access points
- Farther from Toronto than most listed rivers, but worth the drive for trophy wild steelhead
- Low fishing pressure compared to Lake Ontario tribs

## Access Points

> Steelhead blocked by Inglis Falls — all migratory fish staging in lower half of river only (below falls). Above dam opens June 1 (special regulation — verify annually).
