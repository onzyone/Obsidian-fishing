# Etobicoke Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~20–30 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), Chinook salmon, brown trout
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Nov)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lakeshore to Lake Ontario (lower) | No | Standard | Year-round |
| Above Lakeshore (upper) | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Dec 31 (FMZ 16) |

- Standard limits apply on all open sections
- C&R recommended given urban watershed and water quality concerns

## Notes

- Small urban creek flowing through Etobicoke south to Lake Ontario
- Gets steelhead and salmon runs in spring and fall
- Much smaller and more urban than Humber River, one valley to the east
- Banks heavily developed in lower reaches — limited natural corridor
- Limited public access; best access near the mouth at Marie Curtis Park
- Water clarity often poor after rain due to urban runoff — fish still present but challenging
- Not heavily documented; low fishing pressure compared to nearby Humber and Credit
- C&R recommended given urban watershed conditions

## Access Points

> Creek flows Caledon → Brampton → Mississauga → Lake Ontario. Upper Brampton/Caledon sections better character for trout than the urbanized lower reaches.

| Location | Notes | Maps |
|---|---|---|
| Marie Curtis Park (river mouth) | Best public access; parking at park; straddles Etobicoke/Mississauga boundary; fresh-run fish enter here | [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Marie+Curtis+Park+Etobicoke+Ontario) |
| Rexdale area (upstream) | Some road allowance access through urban sections | [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Etobicoke+Creek+Rexdale+Ontario) |
