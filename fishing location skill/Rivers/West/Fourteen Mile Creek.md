# Fourteen Mile Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~30–35 min
**Species:** Chinook salmon (fall), white sucker, creek chub; limited rainbow trout/brown trout
**Seasons:** Standard FMZ 16 season on creek; Oakville bylaw restricts fishing to **Oct 15 – May 15** in-town

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Fourteen Mile Creek is **not** named in FMZ 16 Lake Ontario tributary exceptions — defaults to standard zone season for trout/salmon. Additionally, **Town of Oakville bylaw** restricts creek fishing to Oct 15 – May 15 (opposite of provincial). Verify at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16) and [Town of Oakville](https://www.oakville.ca).

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creek — trout / salmon (provincial) | No | Standard | **4th Sat April – Sept 30 (FMZ 16 default)** |
| Creek within Town of Oakville (bylaw) | — | Standard | **Oct 15 – May 15 only** — fishing prohibited May 16 – Oct 14 |
| Net effect (overlap) | No | Standard | Effectively **4th Sat April – May 15** and **Oct 15 – Sept 30** = narrow legal windows; fall run window mostly closed provincially |
| Lake Ontario (mouth area) | No | Standard | Lake waters open all year |

- Between provincial season and town bylaw there is almost no overlap that actually allows fall-run salmon fishing on the creek — fish the mouth in lake waters instead
- CA: **Conservation Halton**

## Notes

- Small Oakville creek — mouth is between Bronte Creek and Sixteen Mile Creek; third of the "_____-Mile Creek" set (Twelve Mile = Bronte, Fourteen Mile, Sixteen Mile); name refers to distance from western corner of Lake Ontario
- **Environmentally Sensitive Area (ESA)** north of QEW — 250+ plant species, 15+ fish species, low-pressure
- Widely accessible with many trails, especially north of QEW and west of Third Line
- Very small; fall salmon push confined to lower section near the lake
- Not a serious fishery compared to Bronte or Sixteen Mile — alternative spot when those are blown out or crowded
