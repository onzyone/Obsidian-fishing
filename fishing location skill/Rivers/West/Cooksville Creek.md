# Cooksville Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~20–25 min
**Species:** Chinook salmon, rainbow trout/steelhead (fall), brown trout, smallmouth bass, carp
**Seasons:** Mouth/Lake Ontario year-round for warmwater; creek itself standard FMZ 16 season

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Cooksville Creek is **not** named in FMZ 16 Lake Ontario tributary exceptions — defaults to standard zone season for trout/salmon. Verify at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary/fisheries-management-zone-16).

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entire creek (trout / salmon) | No | Standard | **4th Sat April – Sept 30 (FMZ 16 default)** — no legal fall run season on creek |
| Mouth / Lake Ontario waters | No | Standard | Open all year in Lake Ontario itself |

- Fall salmon and steelhead do enter the creek, but legal harvest/fishing on the creek closes Sept 30
- Fish legally at the **mouth at R.K. McMillan Park / Lakefront Promenade** (lake waters) during fall runs
- CA: **Credit Valley Conservation (CVC)**

## Notes

- Urban Mississauga creek — rises near Bristol Rd W and Hurontario St, flows south to Lake Ontario at **R.K. McMillan Park** near Lakeshore Rd and Cawthra Rd
- Heavy stormwater-driven — blows out quickly after rain, slow to clear
- Primary fishery is the mouth and adjacent Lakefront Promenade Park
- Late-August chinook, browns, and rainbows begin staging at the mouth (most fish destined for Credit River to the west)
- Not a serious creek fishery — handy close-to-home spot east of Port Credit
