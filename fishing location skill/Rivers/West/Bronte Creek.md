# Bronte Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~40–45 min
**Species:** Steelhead, migratory brown trout, Chinook salmon
**Seasons:** Spring & Fall runs; some winter steelhead

## Regulations

> Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | Season / Notes |
|---|---|
| **Lakeshore Rd → Rebecca St (Oakville)** | Year-round, but **no shore fishing** — water too deep to wade; boat/kayak only. Halton Police enforce (~$300 fine). |
| **Rebecca St → Lowville Falls (mid-section)** | Open Apr 25 – Dec 31 |
| **CPR track at Progreston → Hwy 407** | **CLOSED (sanctuary):** Jan 1 – Fri before 4th Sat in April, and Oct 1 – Dec 31 |
| **Above Progreston (Carlisle and up)** | Resident brown/brook trout only; no migratory fish above Lowville Falls |

- No catch-and-release zones — standard limits apply outside sanctuary

## Notes

- Smaller river but gets big runs — very productive per volume
- **Lowville Park waterfall** is a natural barrier — all migratory fish stack below it; focus fishing below this point
- Popular river — heavily fished, especially Petro Park. Explore water above Petro Park for fewer people; forum anglers note "a seemingly endless amount of water above it that holds just as much fish"
- Good beginner river due to size
- Spring steelhead run: late winter–early spring, fish 2–15 lbs
- Fall run: mid-October to mid-December
- Extended open season in fall to Dec 31 (lower section only — sanctuary section closes Oct 1)

## Access Points

### Lower River (Lake → Lowville Falls) — Primary Steelhead Water

| Location | Notes | Maps |
|---|---|---|
| **Petro Canada Park (Petro Park)** — mouth area, Bronte Harbour | Main parking at the creek mouth. Very popular and crowded, especially on weekends. Fish push past here early in runs. Road: Bronte Rd / Lakeshore Rd, Oakville. | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Petro+Canada+Park+Bronte+Oakville+Ontario) |
| **Rebecca Street bridge** | Northern boundary of the no-shore-fishing zone. Fishing permitted north of this bridge. | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Rebecca+Street+Bronte+Creek+Oakville+Ontario) |
| **QEW underpass / bridge** | Mentioned by forum anglers as an access point. Walk in from road allowance near QEW crossing. Less pressure than Petro Park. | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=QEW+Bronte+Creek+Oakville+Ontario) |
| **Below Lowville Park waterfall — key holding area** | Natural barrier; all migratory fish stack below falls. Primary steelhead holding spot. Parking at Lowville Park. | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Lowville+Park+Burlington+Ontario) |

### Bronte Creek Provincial Park (between Hwy 403 and QEW)

Park address: **1219 Burloak Drive, Oakville** (QEW Exit 109, go north 1 km). Day-use vehicle permit required (pay-and-display, credit card or coins). Call 905-827-6911.

| Access Option | Parking | Route | Distance to Creek |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tyrrell / Mid-Park** | Parking Lot A | Walk toward Tyrrell B picnic shelter; find "fisherman's path" in tall grass behind shelter; switchbacks down to creek | ~1.5 km |
| **Half Moon Valley Trail / Lower Park** | Parking Lot F *(closed in winter, reopens spring)* | Follow Half Moon Valley Trail, keep right; in lower valley keep left; creek is 100 m ahead | ~2 km |

- The park protects several kilometres of creek between Hwy 403 and the QEW — less crowded than the lower river.
- Fish species: steelhead, rainbow trout, Chinook salmon, splake.
- Steelhead season here typically mid-October through mid-December (fall run). Spring run late winter–early spring.

### Upper River (above Lowville / Hwy 5 / Dundas)

- **Above Lowville Falls:** Migratory fish are blocked by the waterfall. Upper sections hold resident brown and brook trout only — not steelhead water.
- **Progreston (Carlisle) — CPR track:** This is the upstream boundary of the regulated migratory section. A sanctuary zone applies Oct 1–Dec 31 and Jan 1–spring opening.
- Access above the park is largely through private land; public road allowances at bridge crossings (Dundas St / Hwy 5, Upper Middle Rd, Bronte Rd) may offer limited walk-in points but are not confirmed public access — scout in person.
