# Bowmanville Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~55–65 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), Chinook salmon, brown trout, some Atlantic salmon
**Seasons:** Spring (mid-Mar – Apr steelhead); Fall (Sept – Oct salmon, Oct – Nov steelhead)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Season |
|---|---|---|
| General | No | Apr 25 – Dec 31 (FMZ 17) |

- No specific C&R zones — standard FMZ 17 rules apply
- Verify sanctuary rules near fish ladder annually

## Notes

- Bowmanville / Clarington area — east of Toronto off Hwy 401
- **Fish ladder** at Goodyear Dam allows salmon & large steelhead to pass upstream (~1 km north of Baseline Rd)
- Before ladder was built, only ~1% of large Chinook salmon made it past the dam
- Good public access via paved Bowmanville Valley Trail along the creek
- Spring: rainbow trout (steelhead) arrive mid-March to early April
- Fall: Chinook salmon peak September/October; steelhead follow after

# Ganaraska River ("The Ganny")

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 10–20 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), Chinook salmon
**Seasons:** Spring steelhead run (Mar – May) — arguably the **largest spring steelhead run in Ontario**

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|
| **Hwy 401 → Jocelyn St Bridge** | — | **CLOSED year-round** (sanctuary) |
| **Jocelyn St Bridge → CNR right-of-way** | No | Apr 25 – Aug 31; Oct 15 – Dec 31 · **CLOSED** Sept 1 – Oct 14 |
| CNR right-of-way → Lake Ontario | No | Year-round |
| Above Hwy 2 (upper river) | No | Apr 25 – Dec 31 |

- No C&R zones — standard limits apply
- **No night fishing** Aug 15 – Dec 31 (8 PM – 6 AM) in regulated sections
- No snagging

## Notes

- Flows through **Ganaraska Forest** and empties into Lake Ontario at **Port Hope**
- Spring run is legendary — very high fish numbers
- Lots of public water but also lots of angler pressure during peak weeks
- Upper Ganaraska (above dam) has a separate steelhead population accessible by fish ladder

# Graham Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~80–90 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), brown trout, some Chinook salmon
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Nov)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| All sections | No | Standard | Apr 25 – Dec 31 (FMZ 17) |

- Standard limits apply on all open sections
- Small creek — verify any specific section rules in current Ontario regulations before fishing

## Notes

- Small creek in the eastern Lake Ontario watershed (mouth in Newcastle, Clarington / Durham Region; upper reaches extend into Northumberland border)
- Watershed area 78 km²; largest tributary Mulligan Creek (also Crooked Creek, Lytle Creek)
- OSM-logged course: mouth 43.9003, -78.5833 → mid 43.9254, -78.5722 (north of 401) → upper 43.9322, -78.5681
- Low fishing pressure; lightly documented compared to larger eastern tribs
- Gets steelhead in spring; some brown trout present
- Limited public access information available — research road allowances and local conservation area access before the trip
- Check current Ontario regulations for any special sections or sanctuary designations

=== East / Wilmot Creek

# Wilmot Creek

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~55–65 min
**Species:** Steelhead, salmon
**Seasons:** Spring & Fall

> ⚠️ **Size note:** Wilmot Creek is a small, short creek. The year-round fishable section (south of CNR to Lake Ontario) is only a few hundred metres long. It receives run activity but is not a destination fishery — treat it as a quick stop if already driving east, not a primary target.

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Season |
|---|---|---|
| South of CNR tracks → Lake Ontario | No | Year-round |
| Above CNR tracks | No | Apr 25 onward (FMZ 17) |

- No C&R zones — standard FMZ 17 rules apply

## Notes

- Newcastle / Bowmanville area, east of Toronto
- Short lower section only (south of CNR → lake) — limited fishable water
- Gets some steelhead and salmon staging near the mouth in spring and fall
- No live fish counter available
- Run size estimates from third-party sites (e.g. "15,000+ steelhead") are unverified and likely exaggerated for this watershed
- Good public access at Samuel Wilmot Nature Area near the mouth

## Access Points

> Public access runs from Lake Ontario north to approximately Hwy 115. Above Hwy 115 is mostly private — do not access.

| **Wilmot Creek mouth at Lake Ontario** (south of CNR — year-round) | Year-round open below CNR tracks; few hundred metres of fishable water; gauge 02HD009 | [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=43.9015,-78.5860) |
