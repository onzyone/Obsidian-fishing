# St. John's Conservation Area

**Address:** 3101 Barron Rd, Fonthill, ON L0S 1E6
**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~95 km / 1h 15 min (QEW toward Niagara, exit Pelham/Fonthill)
**Managed by:** Niagara Peninsula Conservation Authority (NPCA)

## Access Point

| Location | Maps |
|---|---|
| St. John's Conservation Area — stocked trout pond | [Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=St+Johns+Conservation+Area+3101+Barron+Rd+Fonthill+Ontario) |

## Season & Fees

| | Details |
|---|---|
| **Open Season** | 4th Saturday in April – September 30 (2026: Apr 25 – Sep 30) |
| **Admission** | Free (voluntary $2/person or $5/family donation on opening day) |
| **Licence Required** | Yes — valid Ontario fishing licence |

## Stocking

| Species | Schedule | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rainbow trout | Twice per year — spring (before opener) + mid-season | Man-made spring-fed pond (~0.75 acres), formerly a gravel pit fed by springs off Twelve Mile Creek |

## Special Rules

- No live bait
- Two wheelchair-accessible fishing piers and accessible boardwalk
- Catch and release encouraged (not mandatory)
- FMZ 16 regulations apply
