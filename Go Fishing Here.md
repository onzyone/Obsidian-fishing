### What to Expect

**Spring run:** Early but building. The Ganaraska and Beaver both confirmed first fish Apr 12 — by Apr 24 the run will be 12 days in, which typically means fish moving steadily but not peak numbers yet. The storm this week is actually good news: large rain events followed by dropping/clearing water are classic steelhead migration triggers. Fish pushed hard by the flood will be holding in pools and resting, then moving again on stable dropping water.

**Tactics for Apr 24 conditions:** Water temp likely 8–10°C at Beaver/Ganaraska. Use egg patterns, spawn bags, or small jigs (pink/orange/chartreuse) under a float. Fish will be in the deeper pools below riffles, and in current seams. On larger rivers (Ganaraska, Nottawasaga) try swinging wet flies or small spoons on a falling river — fish are often aggressive on clear, dropping water. Morning fishing best before afternoon warming raises temps above 12°C (trout become less active above 15°C).

**Apr 23 caution:** 26.1°C air temp = water warming fast. Fish early (6–10am), go deep and slow in afternoon. Apr 24's cooler 18.1°C is better all-day fishing.
