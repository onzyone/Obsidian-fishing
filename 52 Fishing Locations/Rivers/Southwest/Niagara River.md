# Niagara River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 30–45 min
**Species:** Trophy steelhead (rainbow trout), large brown trout, Chinook salmon, coho salmon, lake trout (lower river/gorge)
**Seasons:** Year-round (lower sections); peak steelhead Spring (Mar–May) and Fall (Oct–Dec)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower Niagara (Queenston to Lake Ontario) | No (verify slot rules) | Standard | Year-round (FMZ 16) |
| Niagara Gorge (below Falls) | No (verify slot/size) | Standard | Year-round (FMZ 16) |
| Upper Niagara (above Falls — different fishery) | — | — | Verify separately |

- **Canadian fishing licence required for the Ontario (east/north) bank**
- **US fishing licence required for the New York (west/south) bank** — fish Ontario side only unless you hold both licences
- Complex slot/size rules may apply — verify current FMZ 16 regulations annually
- C&R recommended for lake trout (species at risk considerations in this system)

## Notes

- One of the most productive steelhead fisheries in North America due to the volume of fish staging between Lake Erie and Lake Ontario
- The gorge below Niagara Falls holds trophy fish year-round; fish are large and powerful in the current
- Famous for 10–15 lb steelhead — significantly larger fish on average than Lake Ontario tribs
- Primarily drift/float fishing and fly fishing techniques
- **Cross-border complexity:** fish the Ontario side only unless you carry both a Canadian and US fishing licence
- The lower Niagara (Queenston to Lake Ontario) is the most accessible steelhead zone from the Ontario side
- Access is challenging — steep gorge walls require use of staircases at Niagara Glen or paths at Queenston/Lewiston areas
- Niagara Glen access is managed by Niagara Parks Commission — fee may apply
- Upper Niagara (above Falls near Chippawa) is a different fishery — verify separately
