# Grand River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 10–20 min
**Species:** Brown trout (wild), rainbow trout, steelhead, smallmouth bass
**Seasons:** Year-round; spring and fall best for steelhead; summer for resident brown trout

## Notes

- One of Ontario's premier wild trout rivers — particularly renowned for **large wild brown trout**; 25"+ fish caught regularly in the upper tailwater section
- Cambridge / Fergus / Elora area — multiple quality sections; access is very good with plenty of water to spread out even on busy days
- Designated a Canadian Heritage River
- Elora Gorge section is iconic fly fishing water
- Grand River Conservation Authority manages significant public access
- **Best seasons are early (spring) and late (fall)** — summer heat stresses fish and increases C&R mortality; avoid fishing the upper trout section in peak summer heat
- Lower river (Paris → Lake Erie) is big water — longer **spey or switch rods** are recommended for swinging flies effectively

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Belwood Dam → West Montrose (Elora/Fergus)** | ✅ **C&R only** | Artificial lures only · 1 barbless hook | Apr 25 – Sept 30 |
| **Paris → Brantford** | ✅ **C&R only** (trout, pike, bass, walleye) | Artificial lures only · 1 barbless hook | Apr 25 – Dec 31 · **CLOSED** Mar 1 – Fri before 4th Sat April; Oct 1 – Nov 30 (Penman Dam to William St Bridge) |
| Brantford → Lake Erie | No (limit 1 trout Oct–Dec) | Standard | Apr 25 – Dec 31 |

- **Both the trophy brown trout section AND the steelhead section are catch and release** — know which water you're on

## River Sections

| Section | Species | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Belwood Dam → West Montrose (~30 km) | Wild & stocked brown trout | Trophy trout zone — catch & release, barbless, no organic bait. Browns 30"+ documented. |
| West Montrose → Cambridge | Mixed warm/cold water | Wider, shallower — transitions to warm water species |
| Paris → Brantford → Cambridge | Steelhead, steelhead | ~100 km steelhead run; best below dams in fall |

## Steelhead Timing

> **Lower river only** (Paris → Lake Erie, ~100 km). Season open 4th Saturday of April – Dec 31.

| Period | Timing | Conditions | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fall run (peak)** | Mid-Oct – early Dec | Water drops to ~60°F after sustained cold rains; fish push in hard | Paris Dam, Brantford, Caledonia |
| **Early fall** | Late Sept – mid-Oct | Small numbers; fishing can be inconsistent until temps drop | Paris, Cambridge |
| **Late fall** | Nov – Dec 31 | Best numbers, fewest crowds — best time to go | Entire lower river |
| **Spring run** | 4th Sat April – May | Stragglers dropping out of tributaries; lower numbers than fall | Cambridge, Brantford |

**Key trigger:** First sustained cold rains of fall + river temperature hitting ~60°F. Fish best when river is **dropping and clearing** after rain — can take 5–10 days to clear after heavy rain. Avoid fishing a blown-out chocolate river; wait it out.
