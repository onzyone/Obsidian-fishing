# Credit River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~30–40 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), migratory brown trout, Chinook salmon, resident brown trout
**Seasons:** Year-round (lower river); Spring & Fall peak runs

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hwy 403 → Lake Ontario | No | Standard | Year-round |
| Hwy 403 → Britannia Rd | No | Standard | **CLOSED** Jan 1 – Fri before 4th Sat April; Aug 15 – Dec 31 |
| Old Baseline Rd → Hwy 9 | No | Standard | **CLOSED** Jan 1 – Fri before 4th Sat April; Oct 1 – Dec 31 |
| **Above Old Baseline Rd (Caledon)** | ✅ **C&R only** | Artificial lures only · 1 single barbless hook | Apr 25 – Sept 30 |

- **The upper Credit (above Old Baseline Rd) is full catch and release** — no bait, single barbless hook

## Notes

- One of the best trout rivers in the Toronto area — large river with lots of water
- Highway 403 downstream to Lake Ontario open all year for rainbow/brown trout and salmon
- Best resident trout fishing is between **Inglewood and Alton** (upper river)
- Zero trout retention — catch and release only (verify current regs)

## Access Points

> All lower river sections (Hwy 403 → Lake Ontario) have free public parking unless noted.
> Walk distance = approximate one-way from parking lot to fishable water.

### Upper River — Forks of the Credit / Cataract / Inglewood → Alton — C&R Only, Artificial Lures Only

> ⚠️ **C&R only, artificial lures only, single barbless hook, Apr 25 – Sept 30.** This is also wild brown trout country — gin-clear water, light tippet (5–6x), careful approach required.

| Location | Parking | Walk | Water / Notes | Maps |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cataract village bridge (Station Rd / Charleston Sideroad W) | Roadside shoulder near bridge | 0 min | Best free access to the Forks area; walk up or downstream on Crown road allowance; prime pool below the old mill site. **Cataract Falls is the dividing line — brook trout dominate above the falls (colder water), brown trout below** | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cataract+Ontario+Credit+River+bridge) |
| Forks of the Credit Rd — roadside pulloffs | Shoulder pulloffs along Forks of the Credit Rd **before** park fee gate | 2–5 min | Free access; walk down to river from road shoulder; avoid the paid day-use lot | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Forks+of+the+Credit+Road+Caledon+Ontario) |
| McLaughlin Rd bridge | Gravel shoulder | 0 min | Road allowance access; productive riffle-pool run; good brown trout water | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=McLaughlin+Road+Credit+River+Caledon+Ontario) |
| Mississauga Rd N bridge (near Belfountain) | Shoulder pulloff at bridge | 0 min | Road allowance at bridge; upstream of Belfountain CA (paid); walk downstream; mixed riffles | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mississauga+Road+Credit+River+Belfountain+Ontario) |
| Boston Mills Rd bridge | Shoulder pulloff | 0 min | Road allowance; downstream of Forks; transition water between upper and mid river; brown trout and early steelhead | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Boston+Mills+Road+Credit+River+Caledon+Ontario) |
| Old Baseline Rd bridge (regulatory boundary) | Roadside | 0 min | The regulatory dividing line — above here is C&R/artificial only year-round; below has seasonal closures; pools at the bridge hold fish | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Old+Base+Line+Road+Credit+River+Caledon+Ontario) |
| Inglewood village area | Roadside | 2–5 min | Walk down from village; classic small-town pool/riffle access; less pressure than Forks area | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Inglewood+Ontario+Credit+River) |
| Alton / Airport Rd area | Roadside/informal | 5 min | Upper limit of productive brown trout water; cold, tight cover; smaller fish but more isolated | [Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Alton+Ontario+Credit+River) |

**Paid areas to skip:** Forks of the Credit Provincial Park day-use lot (~$12–18/vehicle), Belfountain Conservation Area. The road allowance access points above give the same water for free.

## Hatch Chart — Upper Credit River

*Source: [Ontario Fly Fisher](https://www.ontarioflyfisher.com/credit-river-hatch-chart/) — applies to Forks of the Credit, Cataract, Inglewood, Alton sections.*

> **Note:** Isonychia is described as the most prolific and reliable mayfly on the upper Credit. Terrestrials (ants, grasshoppers) are productive on warm summer/fall days but not listed in the chart.

| Hatch | Hook Size | Peak Months | Suggested Patterns |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Black Stonefly | #16–18 | April | Early Black Stonefly dry |
| Blue Winged Olive (BWO) | #16–20 | April, August | Parachute Adams, RS2, Pheasant Tail Nymph |
| Hendrickson | #12–16 | May | Hendrickson dry, Red Quill dry, PT Nymph |
| Sulphur | #14–18 | May | Sulphur Parachute, CDC Sulphur |
| Grey Fox | #12–14 | May | Comparadun, March Brown Soft Hackle |
| Spotted Sedge | #14–16 | May–June | Elk Hair Caddis, X-Caddis |
| Little Yellow Stonefly | #14–16 | May–June | Yellow Sally |
| Green Drake | #8–10 | May–June | Green Drake Parachute, Woolly Bugger |
| Isonychia ⭐ | #10–12 | June–July | Isonychia Comparadun, Parachute Iso, Leadwing Coachman |
| Trico | #20–24 | June–July | Trico Spinner, CDC Trico — very small, fine tippet required |
| Golden Stonefly | #8–10 | June–July | Stimulator, Golden Stone dry |
| Late Hex | #8–10 | August | Hexagenia Parachute, Hex dry — fish at dusk |

## Fish Counter

| Counter | Type | URL |
|---|---|---|
| CRAA Live Camera — River Watcher | Infrared silhouette counter (live feed) | [craa.on.ca](https://www.craa.on.ca/live-camera-river-watcher/) |

**Location:** Streetsville Dam, Mississauga — on private property owned by Kraft Canada. The fish ladder and counter are operated by CRAA volunteers in partnership with OMNRF. Monitors steelhead, Atlantic salmon, and brown trout moving upstream through the fishway. No public access to the counter site itself — view data online only.
