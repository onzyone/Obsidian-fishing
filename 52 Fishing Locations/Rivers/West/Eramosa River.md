# Eramosa River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~1h 10 min (Guelph area)
**Species:** Brown trout (resident), brook trout (upper), rainbow trout (some stocked sections)
**Seasons:** Year-round (lower); Spring peak for resident fish activity

## Why Fish This Over the Main Credit or Humber

The Eramosa is **groundwater-fed through limestone and glacial moraine geology**, making it one of the clearest rivers in southwestern Ontario after heavy rain. When the Credit and Humber are blown out:

- Groundwater springs maintain consistent baseflow and filter silt
- Limestone bedrock and karst features provide natural filtration
- Clears in **1–2 days** after a rain event; may not spike noticeably at all during moderate rain
- Cooler summer temperatures than agricultural rivers — fish later into the season

**Best case to go here:** Credit and Humber are murky; you want resident brown or brook trout on dries or small spinners; you're OK with a 70-min drive; no steelhead run to worry about.

## Notes

- Flows east through the Guelph area and joins the **Speed River**, which joins the Grand River at Cambridge
- **No migratory fish** — the Grand/Speed/Eramosa system is landlocked above the Dundas Dam; targets are resident brown trout and brookies only
- Small-medium stream — wade-able in most sections (knee-to-thigh deep)
- Upper sections (above Guelph reservoir) are tighter, more brook-trout character; lower sections through Guelph are wider with better brown trout water
- Grand River Conservation Authority (GRCA) manages the watershed
- Spring creek character: fish can be spooky on clear days — approach carefully; long casts, tippets down to 4x or 5x
- **Limestone geology** means water stays cool even in summer — fish actively longer than clay-bottom streams

## Run Timing

- No migratory run
- **Resident brown trout** active spring through fall; best in April–June when post-spawn fish are feeding hard
- **Brook trout** in upper sections best in late spring and early fall
- Summer: fish early morning or evening when temps cool; water stays clearer than comparable rivers

## Wading & Conditions

- Generally easy wading — flat limestone shelves, gravel runs, not technical
- Gorge section at Rockwood requires care — slippery limestone ledges, drop pools
- Low water in July–August can make wading unnecessary (fish from bank)
- After rain: Eramosa stays clear while Credit/Humber spike — ideal time to visit
