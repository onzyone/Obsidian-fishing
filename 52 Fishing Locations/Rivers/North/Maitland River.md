# Maitland River

**Drive from 427 & Finch:** ~2h – 2h 15 min
**Species:** Steelhead (rainbow trout), brown trout, Chinook salmon
**Seasons:** Spring (Mar–May), Fall (Oct–Nov)

## Regulations

> ⚠️ Always verify current regs at [ontario.ca](https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-fishing-regulations-summary) before fishing.

| Section | C&R? | Gear Rules | Season / Sanctuary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower section (Goderich / river mouth area) | No | Standard | Extended or year-round — verify FMZ 23 |
| Upper sections | No | Standard | Standard season (FMZ 23) |

- Standard limits apply on all open sections
- Verify lower section extended/year-round designation in current FMZ 23 regulations annually

## Notes

- Flows into Lake Huron at Goderich
- Part of the Lake Huron trib cluster alongside the Saugeen River
- Large, agricultural river — water can be murky/turbid after rain; fish still present but visibility reduced
- Goderich harbour and lower river near the lighthouse/pier area is the prime steelhead access zone
- Known for big fish entering from Lake Huron
- Huron County Conservation Authority manages access points
- Less celebrated than the Saugeen but a good alternate option on the same Lake Huron trip
- Goderich town is scenic and worth a visit (famous for its octagonal town square)
