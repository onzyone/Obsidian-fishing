### Weather & River Summary

Friday April 10 brings a 6.1mm rain event and cold temps (high 5.4°C) — rivers will spike and blow out. Saturday April 11 is the first dry day (0mm, 10.2°C) with rivers dropping from the Friday bump — classic post-rain stacking conditions. Sunday April 12 continues dry but colder (6.3°C high) with rivers further clearing. Monday April 13 is the warmest day of the window (23.3°C) with only 2.6mm drizzle — a strong warm trigger for upstream fish movement, and rivers should be well-cleared by then. Both Credit (Streetsville gauge: falling, fishable) and Humber (Weston gauge: falling, fishable) confirm rivers are on a solid dropping trend heading into the weekend.

### What to Expect

Rainbow trout / steelhead are the primary target with early browns possible. Post-rain: fish will be holding in deeper pools, inside bends, and slow tail-outs below any riffle or barrier. Water will be slightly coloured — go bright (pink eggs, flame/chartreuse streamers, pink San Juan worms). Float/drift rig effective in pools; swing bright streamers through runs. Saturday morning is the best window before afternoon crowds arrive. If skipping Saturday, Monday April 13's 23.3°C high is a genuine warm trigger that could fire fish movement — rivers will be fully cleared by then.
