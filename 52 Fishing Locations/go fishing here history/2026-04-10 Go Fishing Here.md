Rivers are currently dropping from a recent elevated event — Credit (Streetsville gauge) and Humber (Weston gauge) both confirm falling, fishable conditions as of this morning. Friday brings 1.7mm of light showers that won't meaningfully disrupt the falling trend. Saturday is the sweet spot: dry (0mm), cool (8°C), rivers continuing to drop and clear — classic post-rain fish stacking conditions. Sunday is a 7.5mm rain event that will push rivers back up and blow them out. Monday adds another 2.1mm on top with a warm 20°C high, but rivers will still be running dirty and high from Sunday's rain.

### What to Expect

Early spring steelhead and brown trout in the lower Credit. Post-rain conditions: fish holding in deeper pools and slow tail-outs below riffles. Water will be slightly coloured but clearing Saturday morning — go bright (pink eggs, flame/chartreuse streamers, pink San Juan worms). Float/drift rig in the deeper pools; swing bright streamers through runs. Arrive early Saturday before pressure builds and the fishing slows.
