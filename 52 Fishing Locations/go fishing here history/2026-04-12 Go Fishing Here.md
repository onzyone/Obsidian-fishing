### Best Day: Saturday April 18

**Top pick:** Credit River lower
- Why: Was in a falling trend before the storm, will be 2 full days into a drop by Saturday — exactly the "dropping and clearing" window when steelhead feed most aggressively. Slightly coloured water is very fishable with the right presentation. Go at first light.
- Access: Erindale Park, Dundas St W, Mississauga — free, large lot — [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Erindale+Park+Mississauga)
- Drive: 30–40 min from 427 & Finch

**Backup:** Beaver River (Thornbury)
- Why: Limestone escarpment clears it fastest of any river in the region; early run already confirmed on the Biotactic counter; if you arrive at the Credit and it's still too dirty, Beaver is the call — just budget 1h 40+ min.
- Access: Thornbury fish ladder / Beaver River mouth — [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Thornbury+Beaver+River+Ontario)
- Drive: ~1h 40–50 min

### What to Expect

Spring steelhead (rainbow trout) are the target — mid-April is mid-run, just before peak. On a dropping, slightly coloured river go bright: chartreuse, pink, orange. Float fishing with egg beads (pink/peach), yarn flies, or small jigs under an indicator is the move on the lower Credit. Plan to be on the water by 7am and wrap up by early afternoon ahead of any Sunday system. Dress in layers — 24°C air but river water still cold (6–10°C). Polarized glasses worth wearing even in off-colour water.
