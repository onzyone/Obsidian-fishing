### Weather & River Summary

A heavy 3-day storm Tue Apr 14 – Thu Apr 16 (~36.6mm total: 19.4mm Tue, 10.5mm Wed, 6.8mm Thu) will blow out all West-area rivers. Friday Apr 17 is the first dry day after the storm (0mm, 17°C) — perfect weather but Credit and West rivers will be at or near peak height on Day 1 post-storm. The key exception is the **Niagara River (Southwest):** fed by Lake Erie, it doesn't respond to local storm runoff and remains fishable throughout the weekend — Friday's clear skies make it the prime pick. Saturday Apr 18 (19°C, 4.5mm drizzle) sees West rivers dropping; small-watershed Southwest creeks like Twenty Mile may begin clearing. **Sunday Apr 19** is when Credit River enters its recovery window (Day 3 from peak) — slightly coloured and dropping — but the near-freezing overnight low (0.2°C) means a cold morning start.

### Best Day: Friday April 17

**Top pick river:** Niagara River (Niagara Glen / Queenston)
- Why: The only West/Southwest river that is storm-immune this weekend. Friday delivers the best weather of the three days (0mm, 17°C, mostly clear) and the gorge is guaranteed fishable. Spring is prime timing for steelhead and brown trout near the tributary mouths along the lower gorge. Longer drive but zero risk of blown-out water.
- Access: Niagara Glen Nature Reserve, River Rd, Queenston — free parking, trail walk-in to river — [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Niagara+Glen+Nature+Reserve+Ontario)
- Drive: ~1h 35–45 min from 427 & Finch

**Backup (shorter drive — go Sunday):** Credit River lower
- Why: Day 3 from rain peak = entering the clearing window. Slightly coloured and dropping is exactly when steelhead feed hardest. Best on Sunday April 19, not Friday.
- Access: Erindale Park, Dundas St W, Mississauga — free, large lot — [Google Maps](https://maps.google.com/?q=Erindale+Park+Mississauga)
- Drive: 30–40 min from 427 & Finch

### What to Expect

**Niagara River gorge (Friday):** Target current seams and eddy lines in the gorge, and the mouths of any side tributaries that drain into the gorge. Steelhead and brown trout use these tributary confluences as staging areas. Fast, deep water — bring heavier gear (8–10wt or spinning with 15lb fluoro), egg sacs, spawn bags, or large spoons/plugs. Expect a steep trail walk-in at the Glen. Polarized glasses are essential. Spring crowds possible Friday if weather is this nice — arrive early.

**Credit River lower (Sunday):** Post-flood steelhead stack in deep pools and tail-outs below any structure. Water temp ~6–8°C — fish slow and deep. Bright colours (chartreuse, pink, orange) cut through slightly coloured water. Egg beads, yarn flies, or jigs under a float. Dress in layers — 0.2°C overnight means icy guides at first light and frost on the banks. 7am start ideal.
